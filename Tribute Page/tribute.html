Arnold Palmer

"The King"

Arnold Palmer, who made golf popular for the masses with his hard-charging style
  • 1929 - Born September 10th in Latrobe, Pennsylvania
  • 1947 - Accepted golf scholorship to Wake Forest University
  • 1950 - Left Wake Forest for wartime service. Joins U.S. Coast Guard
  • 1954 - Wins United States Amateur Championship
  • 1954 - Marries Winnifred Walzer on December 20
  • 1954 - Turns professional, signs with Wilson Sporting Goods
  • 1955 - Wins first professional tournament, The Canadian Open
  • 1958 - Wins first Masters Tournament
  • 1960 - Wins second Masters Tournament
  • 1960 - Wins first and only US Open
  • 1961 - Wins first Open Championship (British Open)
  • 1962 - Wins third Masters Tournament
  • 1962 - Wins second Open Championship (British Open)
  • 1964 - Wins fourth Masters Tournament
  • 1970 - Awarded honorary LL.D. from Wake Forest University
  • 1974 - Voted into World Golf Hall of Fame, Original Inductee
  • 1974 - Purchased Bay Hill Club and Lodge (Bay Hill, FL)
  • 1989 - Opens Arnold Palmer Hospital for Children & Women at Orlando Regional Medical Center
  • 1992 - Establishes major annual fundraiser for Latrobe Area Hospital
  • 2004 - Receives Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2006 - Opens Winnie Palmer Hospital for Women & Babies at Orlando Health
  • 2009 - Receives Congressional Gold Medal
  • 2016 - Passes away, September 25 (aged 87) Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

"Swing your swing. Not some idea of a swing. Not a swing you saw on TV. Not that swing you wish you had. No, swing your swing. Capable of greatness. Prized only by you. Perfect in it's imperfection. Swing your swing. I know, I did."

-- Arnold Palmer
